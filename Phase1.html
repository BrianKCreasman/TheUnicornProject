Phase 1

Laying the Groundwork: Current Conditions, Coherence, and Readiness

Phase 1 establishes the essential foundation for any district implementing a Local Accountability System. It ensures that districts begin with a shared understanding of their current conditions, systemic readiness, guiding principles, and the evidence required to inform Phase 2 system design. This phase embeds the key framework principles of systemic coherence, instructional relevance, structured adaptability, evidence-based reflexive monitoring, equity, transparency, leadership commitment, and calibration.

Phase 1 Narrative Overview

Phase 1 is strategic, diagnostic, and deeply reflective. It prepares leaders, educators, and community partners to enter the design process with clarity rather than assumptions. Districts begin by identifying existing instructional, cultural, and operational conditions through a comprehensive readiness analysis that examines resource structures, assessment systems, professional learning, instructional practices, community expectations, and student experience data.

During this process, teams examine coherence across curriculum, instruction, assessment, and resource allocation. This step directly addresses the Area for Improvement: the phase does not explicitly link findings to a hierarchy of student outcomes by requiring every evidence source to be mapped to the district’s student success outcomes, including academic, real-world, durable-skills, well-being, and readiness indicators.

Phase 1 now includes a Systemic Coherence Scan, a structured tool that ensures schools and districts identify where alignment exists and where fragmentation persists across curriculum, instruction, assessment, and student-support systems. To address the Area for Improvement: components feel isolated rather than connected, districts complete a Phase 1 Outcome Alignment Summary and a visual Phase 1 Coherence Map that show how current practices support (or fail to support) desired outcomes. These are core Recommended Changes that make coherence visible, documented, and ready to inform Phase 2 architecture.

Instructional relevance is a core requirement. Current research emphasizes that the value of accountability rests on its utility for teachers and students, not its compliance function. To respond to the Area for Improvement: the phase does not identify instructional implications, Phase 1 introduces Instructional Impact Prompts in all analysis tools. Districts must generate concrete statements such as: “What does this condition imply for instruction?” and “What would a teacher need tomorrow as a result of this finding?” A Teacher-Level Insights Summary is also required, addressing the Area for Improvement: no mechanism ensures data is translated into classroom action.

Structured adaptability is central to scalability. Phase 1 now defines Core Requirements (the What) – readiness analysis, coherence mapping, evidence disaggregation, guiding principles verification – and Flexible Components (the How), ensuring districts may adapt tools or engagement structures without compromising integrity. This directly responds to the Area for Improvement: non-negotiables are not clearly stated. A Fidelity-with-Flexibility Rubric (a key Recommended Change) supports consistent understanding of what must remain constant across districts versus what can be customized.

Evidence-based monitoring is strengthened through an embedded Mini-PDSA Cycle (Plan → Do → Study → Act), addressing the Area for Improvement: Phase 1 lacks reflexive monitoring or iterative processes. Teams must Gather, Analyze, Validate, and Reflect on their Phase 1 findings. Each district produces a Phase 1 Reflection Memo as a Recommended Change, documenting emergent insights and early hypotheses about future system design.

Equity is now a non-negotiable analytic lens. To address the Area for Improvement: Phase 1 does not explicitly address equity or disaggregate evidence, teams complete an Equity Readiness Scan that includes subgroup disaggregation, opportunity-to-learn analysis, barrier identification, and equity-focused stakeholder reflection. This ensures Phase 1 identifies disproportionate experiences and supports Phase 2 in building responsive system architecture.

Transparency and communication expectations are explicit. Responding to the Area for Improvement: there is no communication protocol, Phase 1 requires a formal Transparency Protocol documenting how findings will be shared internally and publicly. Districts also produce a Phase 1 Community Summary Report, a key Recommended Change that ensures community-facing communication is clear, accessible, and multi-channel.

Leadership commitment is essential for Phase 1 integrity. To address the Area for Improvement: leadership commitment is not codified, districts must complete a Leadership Commitment Statement, signed by district and school leaders, outlining resources, time allocations, and expectations for participation. A Shared Ownership Map clarifies who is responsible for each Phase 1 component and supports durable implementation.

Finally, implementation reliability is strengthened through calibration. Responding to the Area for Improvement: lack of calibration protocols, Phase 1 includes a Calibration Protocol, a Phase 1 Evidence Rating Guide, and required team training. These Recommended Changes ensure consistent interpretation of readiness indicators, reduce variation in data quality, and build trust in the evidence base that anchors Phases 2–6.

Collectively, these Recommended Changes ensure Phase 1 functions as a powerful, coherent, equity-centered diagnostic engine that prepares districts for high-quality system design in Phase 2 while remaining scalable, sustainable, and rooted in lived, local reality.

Phase 1 – Principle-Based Design

Phase 1 is deliberately aligned to the Framework Principle Criteria: Systemic Coherence & Outcome Alignment (SCOA), Instructional Relevance & Actionability (IRA), Structured Adaptability & Protection of Core (SAPC), Evidence-Based Reflexive Monitoring (EBRM), Equitable Design & Impact (EDI), Stakeholder Transparency & Communication (STC), Leadership Commitment & Sustained Ownership (LCSO), and Implementation Reliability & Calibration (IRC).

Key Phase 1 tools and outputs:

  • SCOASystemic Coherence Scan, Phase 1 Outcome Alignment Summary, and Phase 1 Coherence Map
  • IRAInstructional Impact Prompts and Teacher-Level Insights Summary
  • SAPCCore Requirements (the What), Flexible Components (the How), and Fidelity-with-Flexibility Rubric
  • EBRMMini-PDSA Cycle and Phase 1 Reflection Memo
  • EDIEquity Readiness Scan and disaggregated evidence expectations
  • STCTransparency Protocol and Phase 1 Community Summary Report
  • LCSOLeadership Commitment Statement and Shared Ownership Map
  • IRCCalibration Protocol and Phase 1 Evidence Rating Guide

Phase 1 – Required Outputs

  • Phase 1 Outcome Alignment Summary
  • Phase 1 Coherence Map
  • Equity Readiness Scan and disaggregated data tables
  • Teacher-Level Insights Summary
  • Phase 1 Reflection Memo (Mini-PDSA documentation)
  • Leadership Commitment Statement and Shared Ownership Map
  • Transparency Protocol and Phase 1 Community Summary Report
  • Calibration Protocol and Phase 1 Evidence Rating Guide

These outputs become the required bridge into Phase 2: Designing the Vision and Architecture of the Local Accountability System.

Phase 1 Checklists for Leaders & Users

Use these checklists as you begin your Local Accountability journey. They are organized by the Six Pillars, the Local Accountability Continuum, and the DDIER cycle.

Checklist A – Six Pillars

1. Purpose & Theory of Action
  • Guiding Principles reviewed and affirmed
  • Systemic Coherence Scan completed
  • Phase 1 Outcome Alignment Summary produced
  • Phase 1 Coherence Map documented
  • Leadership Commitment Statement signed
2. Governance & Leadership
  • Shared Ownership Map completed
  • Leadership roles and responsibilities documented
  • Protected time allocated for Phase 1 activities
3. Community Engagement & Advocacy
  • Transparency Protocol established
  • Phase 1 Community Summary Report drafted
  • Stakeholder listening sessions and feedback activities conducted
4. Data Validation & Utilization
  • Equity Readiness Scan completed
  • All evidence disaggregated by student subgroup
  • Calibration Protocol executed
  • Phase 1 Evidence Rating Guide applied
5. Evaluating Progress & Impact
  • Mini-PDSA Cycle for Phase 1 completed
  • Phase 1 Reflection Memo written
  • Instructional Impact Prompts responses documented
  • Teacher-Level Insights Summary created
6. Reporting & Transparency
  • Public-facing documents prepared using plain language
  • Findings shared via multi-channel communication (web, meetings, print)
  • Internal and external feedback loops established

Checklist B – Local Accountability Continuum

1. Define Success for Students
  • Student success outcomes clarified and documented
  • Gaps between desired and actual student experiences identified
  • Findings incorporated into the Phase 1 Outcome Alignment Summary
2. Design Vibrant Learning Experiences
  • Instructional Impact Prompts completed for key findings
  • Teacher-Level Insights Summary reflects concrete instructional implications
3. Demonstrate Student Success
  • Evidence sources mapped to student outcomes and equity metrics
  • Readiness analysis identifies performance gaps across student groups
4. Define Success for the Local School District
  • System-level readiness evaluated (governance, staffing, resources)
  • Fragmentation and coherence documented in the Phase 1 Coherence Map
5. Design an Interactive Local Accountability System
  • Phase 1 outputs assembled as required inputs for Phase 2 design
  • Core Requirements (the What) and Flexible Components (the How) identified
6. Demonstrate Success of the Local School District
  • Transparency Protocol and public reporting expectations defined
  • Equity and calibration foundations in place to support later measures of district success

Checklist C – DDIER Cycle

Design
  • Guiding Principles for Local Accountability confirmed and documented
  • Intended Phase 1 outputs defined and communicated to teams
Development
  • Systemic Coherence Scan and evidence collection tools developed or selected
  • Equity Readiness Scan tools and subgroup disaggregation structures prepared
Implementation
  • Teams trained using the Calibration Protocol and Phase 1 Evidence Rating Guide
  • Stakeholder engagement activities (listening sessions, surveys, focus groups) implemented
Evaluation
  • Mini-PDSA Cycle completed for Phase 1 processes
  • Phase 1 Reflection Memo synthesizes insights, tensions, and early decisions
Revision
  • Adjustments identified for Phase 2 design based on Phase 1 findings
  • Feedback from stakeholders and implementation teams incorporated into revised readiness profile
