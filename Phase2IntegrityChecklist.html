Phase 2 Integrity Checklist

Designing the Vision: Architecture That Holds Under Pressure

1) Claims and Definitions

We translated the Portrait of a Learner and Growth/Mastery/Readiness into explicit system claims.
Each claim is written in plain language that a student, family member, and board member can paraphrase.
We defined what the system will not claim (to prevent overreach and misuse).

2) Evidence Logic

Every claim has identified evidence sources (student work, performance evidence, portfolios, academic measures as appropriate).
We flagged evidence that is high-stakes but low-quality (not ready for consequential use).
We defined minimum evidence expectations across grade spans to prevent “random acts of evidence.”

3) Decision Rules and Guardrails

We wrote decision rules for how evidence is used (instruction, support, grading, reporting, accountability).
We have a clear “no single measure defines readiness” guardrail.
Conflicting evidence triggers inquiry and support, not labeling.

4) Governance and Cadence

Roles are clear: who validates evidence, who interprets it, who decides, who monitors drift.
We defined a cadence for evidence review, calibration, stakeholder loops, and reporting.
We built a decision-log routine (what changed, why, and what evidence informed it).

5) Reporting by Design

Reporting is organized around Growth, Mastery, and Readiness, not a single composite score.
We created public explainers: what we measure, why it matters, what strong evidence looks like, and how to improve.
We separated internal improvement reporting from public understanding reporting so each remains usable.

6) Phase 3 Readiness

We converted evidence gaps into a prioritized build list (tasks, rubrics, calibration, portfolios, dashboards).
We defined pilot logic: where to start, what to collect, and how scaling decisions will be made.
We defined integrity checks that indicate drift early, before scaling.
Non-negotiable reality: If you cannot confidently check most items, do not move to Phase 3 yet. Phase 3 will not fix design ambiguity. It will amplify it.
