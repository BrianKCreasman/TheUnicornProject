Phase 2 on a Page. Designing Accountability Architecture
Evidence and Learning Loop Reporting and Transparency Governance and Integrity
Goal. Turn Phase 1 findings into a clear, usable accountability architecture that defines what counts as evidence, how it gets validated, and how it gets reported in a way the community can understand and trust.
In plain language
  • We tighten Growth, Mastery, and Readiness into definitions that can be observed and defended.
  • We decide what counts as evidence, what quality looks like, and how we protect credibility.
  • We design simple routines for collecting, reviewing, and sharing evidence without creating paperwork.
  • We build reporting that is understandable to families and defensible to professionals.
By the end of Phase 2, we have
  • A Phase 2 Design Package that describes the full system architecture.
  • A coherent claim-evidence map plus a clear evidence taxonomy.
  • Quality criteria, exemplars, and a calibration routine that fits PLC time.
  • Draft reporting templates and interpretation guides for non-educators.
  • Decision rules and governance routines that prevent drift.
  • A pilot-ready blueprint aligned to Phase 3.
Who needs to understand this
  • Board and district leaders who protect integrity and coherence.
  • School and teacher leaders who operationalize evidence routines.
  • Students, families, and community partners who need clarity and trust in reporting.
Phase 2 success test
If you can explain the architecture in plain language, teach it to staff, and defend it publicly without shifting back to a scoreboard, you are ready to pilot.

          2. Preconditions and Readiness to Launch Phase 2
          Confirm minimum conditions are in place before asking teams to build Phase 2 outputs.
          Minimum District Readiness
          Before design sprints
          District leaders should confirm these items before launching Phase 2 work.
          • Phase 1 synthesis and 3 to 5 district design priorities are clear.
          • Phase 2 design team owners are assigned (definitions, evidence, reporting, governance).
          • Decision log template and a decision cadence exist.
          • Artifact repository structure and naming conventions exist.
          • Plan to test drafts with educators, students, families, and partners is scheduled.
          • Time is protected for calibration and interpretability testing.
          • Minimum viable design is defined to prevent scope creep.
          LAAC focus in this stage
          LAAC members are oriented to Phase 2 decisions, how feedback will be used, and what will be shared back publicly.

          3. Roles, Responsibilities, and the LAAC
          Clear lanes for Phase 2 design so the system stays coherent and the community stays included.
          System Level Roles
          District and board
          Superintendent and district leaders
          • Protect coherence. Limit scope. Keep decisions disciplined.
          • Own the decision log and publish ‚Äúwhat we decided and why‚Äù summaries.
          • Ensure evidence quality and calibration routines are realistic.
          • Coordinate LAAC testing sessions and share back outcomes.
          • Prepare board learning moments focused on interpretability and integrity.
          Board members
          • Learn the system claims and what evidence supports each claim.
          • Ask questions about equity, credibility, and public understanding.
          • Protect policy and resources that make the system implementable.
          • Support messaging that avoids rankings and scoreboards.
          School and Classroom Roles
          School and classroom level
          Principals and leadership teams
          • Test feasibility. Translate design into school routines.
          • Lead staff calibration using real student work.
          • Identify barriers early and log them.
          • Protect equity of opportunity so evidence is not distorted.
          Teachers and staff
          • Test evidence criteria against real tasks and student work.
          • Identify which routines fit PLCs and which need redesign.
          • Help build exemplars and non-exemplars for clarity.
          Students and families
          • Stress test whether evidence rules match lived experience.
          • Verify reporting templates are understandable and fair.
          • Help identify where communication will break down.
          Local Accountability Advisory Council (LAAC) in Phase 2
          Co-design and legitimacy
          Purpose
          The LAAC ensures Phase 2 design is locally owned, understandable, and aligned to community values.
          • Review and stress test definitions, evidence rules, and reporting prototypes.
          • Surface interpretability issues before public reporting begins.
          • Flag equity risks and access-to-opportunity gaps that impact evidence.
          • Recommend revisions and help communicate decisions back to the community.
          Expectations
          • LAAC meets at least monthly during Phase 2 design work.
          • Each meeting reviews a tangible draft (definitions, rubric, reporting mockup).
          • District publishes ‚Äúwhat we decided and why‚Äù within two weeks.
          • LAAC members test interpretability with peers and report back themes.

          Leadership Huddle. 20 Minutes to Keep Phase 2 on Track
          Tight, artifact-driven, and repeatable. This is how you keep Phase 2 from drifting into vague updates.
          Huddle Agenda
          20 minutes
          1) What decision are we making this week
          • Name the design decision in one sentence.
          • Confirm who decides and what input is needed.
          2) What artifact are we using to decide
          • One draft definition, rubric row, or reporting mockup.
          • One example and one non-example.
          3) What did stakeholders tell us
          • One clarity signal and one confusion signal.
          • What we will share back, and when.
          4) What is the next build action
          • Assign an owner and a due date.
          • Log the decision and rationale.
          Non-negotiable output
          A one-paragraph decision log entry with the artifact link, rationale, and next action.
          Huddle Prompts
          Use as-is
          • What claim are we making, and what evidence proves it.
          • Where could this design create inequity of opportunity.
          • What part of this would a parent misunderstand and why.
          • What is the minimum viable version that still protects integrity.
          • What will we stop doing because of this design.
          Watch for drift
          If huddles turn into updates without artifacts, stop. Phase 2 is a build cycle, not a committee cycle.
