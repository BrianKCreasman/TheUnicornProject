Kentucky Local Accountability Framework

Phase 1: Laying the Groundwork

This phase helps districts slow down and understand current reality. Leaders examine how learning, assessment, and accountability actually function today. The goal is clarity, not judgment.

  • Surface current practices and assumptions
  • Engage stakeholders in honest reflection
  • Form the Local Accountability Advisory Council

Phase 2: Designing the Vision

This is the design studio. Districts translate insights from Phase 1 into a clear architecture. Decisions are made about what matters, how success is defined, and what evidence counts.

  • Define the theory of action
  • Align Six Pillars and Continuum
  • Draft measures of quality

Phase 3: Development and Testing

In this phase, the framework comes to life. Tools, rubrics, dashboards, and routines are developed and tested with real classrooms and schools.

  • Create prototypes and tools
  • Pilot with early adopters
  • Collect feedback and refine

Phase 4: Implementation and Learning

Implementation is not a finish line. Districts launch the system, support leaders and teachers, and continuously learn from the work.

  • Scale implementation
  • Monitor evidence in real time
  • Revise through continuous improvement
