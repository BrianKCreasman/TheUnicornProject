How to read this diagram

Pick a phase, then look down the column to see four common starting points districts can have within that same phase. Click any card to open a deeper description and practical examples of what that starting point often looks like.

This is designed for real planning. A district can be “Ready to Accelerate” in one phase while still “Emerging” in another. Use the cards to name reality without shame, then decide the next move with clarity.

Common “misreads” to avoid

  • Phase skipping: rushing into dashboards or new measures before defining success and evidence rules.
  • Pilot traps: staying in pilots forever without calibration, comparability, and decision rights.
  • Overbuilding: too many indicators that dilute attention and create reporting fatigue.
  • Trust gaps: scoring and moderation are not technical extras. They are the trust engine.

How to use this with leaders

  • Pick one phase. Ask, “Which starting point best matches our reality, and why?”
  • Identify the highest leverage constraint (capacity, coherence, calibration, communication, governance).
  • Commit to one “next move” that reduces risk and increases clarity.
  • Repeat quarterly. Movement across starting points is progress worth tracking.
